Birthing practices around the world are changing. As women become more educated about their bodies, their babies and birthing and as they become more aware of their rights as a consumer and as a respected person in society, they are demanding change.

Despite this, many women still do not feel comfortable or capable of voicing their concerns or complaints, thinking it is impossible, too hard or not within their rights to challenge or question a doctor. Additionally many women feel and are told that if their baby is healthy then the way they were treated is justified.

How a woman is treated in birth is important to her and her baby as how she feels directly impacts her ability to mother well. Medical negligence, lack of informed consent and rape in birth is often in the form of:

1. Incorrect diagnosis of an emergency, leading to unnecessary medical interventions or caesarean surgery.
2. A fabrication of an emergency by incorrect use of technology and a language of fear.
3. Treatment given without informed consent, omission of information, over-riding a refusal of treatment, misrepresentation of a medical situation. While the majority of courts have held that the state may not force women to undergo unwanted cesareans many women continue to find that they are pressured or coerced into such procedures, or they find that no health care provider will support their decision to have a vaginal birth after a c-section ("VBAC"). Additionally often it is not made clear which birthing practices a doctor follows, thus it is difficult for women to correctly choose the doctor which suits their birth beliefs. Worst still some may listen and agree and accept a womanâ€™s birth plans but do something very different at the time of birth.
4. Rude, abusive or violent treatment. Abusive behavior toward women, especially in childbirth, is unacceptable and harmful. Includes threats, scolding, slapping and hitting, being tied down or forced to lie down, coercion, yelling, belittling, lying, manipulating, mocking, dismissing, refusing to acknowledge, It is known as Birth Rape! THE CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT ENSHRINES

THE FOLLOWING RIGHTS OF CONSUMER
  • Right to be protected from hazardous goods and services
  • Right to be informed about the quality and performance of goods and services
  • Rights to free choice of goods and services
  • Right to be heard in decision making process concerning consumer interests
  • Right to redressal if consumer rights are infringed
  • Right to consumer education

THE PATIENT AS A CONSUMER
Traditionally, patients in India have unquestioning trust in their doctors. Most doctors deserve it. But in some cases, medical negligence has resulted in severe harm physical, mental and financial. In addition, unqualified practitioners have brought suffering to gullible patients. Doctors have been liable to prosecution in civil court, but few malpractice victims sue for compensation, fearing years (even decades) of costly litigation. Fortunately, in 1995 the Supreme Court decreed the medical profession to be a "service" under the Consumer Protection Act, 1986. It set aside a writ Petition challenging the same by the Indian medical Association.

WHAT IS MEDICAL NEGLIGENCE?
Medical negligence is defined as a failure to exercise reasonable skill and care in diagnosis and treatment as per the prevalent standards as that particular point of time. An aggrieved patient who believes that he is a victim of medical negligence can now approach the Consumer Courts for fair compensation, and expect results in a relatively shorter period of two to three years. The procedure is comparatively simple and inexpensive.
Complaints up to 2 years old can be accepted.
